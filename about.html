About Us

Central Zoo was opened in 1906[1]. Over time the zoo was expanded and upgraded, and committed itself to a future in environmental education, bringing animals and people closer together. Historically, Central Zoo operated under the auspices of the Karachi City Council. However, in June 2003 the zoo became a charitable trust and is now governed by a board of six trustees, with the Karachi City Council as principal source of funds. Central Zoo's current group of chimpanzee originate from a male named Tom and two females named Yoka and Sarah. Yoka gave birth to daughters Bebe in 1962, Jodie in 1977 and Jessie in 1978 while Sarah gave birth to a son, Sam, in 1977. Bebe then bred with Tom to produce two sons, Boyd in 1978 and Marty in 1987. Central Zoo used to hold chimpanzee tea parties which were phased out during the early 1960s
